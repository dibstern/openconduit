Conduit — Mark: Pipe Bend

An L-shaped pipe with a smooth radiused corner, like precision plumbing viewed from the side. Data enters from the top, flows down, turns the rounded bend, and exits to the right. The bend is the conduit — two parallel L-shaped walls creating the channel where pixel blocks flow. The vertical segment is pink-tinted, the horizontal segment is cyan-tinted, with a smooth color transition around the radiused corner — 49 of 52.

Design Intent
The pipe bend is the most literal expression of “conduit” — a precision-engineered channel that redirects flow. Data enters vertically and exits horizontally. The smooth radius communicates control and quality; no sharp corners, no turbulence. The color transition from pink to cyan around the bend visualizes the transformation that happens in transit.
